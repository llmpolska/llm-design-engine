# Creative pipeline

The creative director is intentionally a sequence of explicit derivations.

## 1. Product domain

Name the real operating context: restaurant service, clinical triage, field logistics, archival research, or another concrete domain. Do not replace it with “SaaS.”

## 2. User tension

Describe what becomes difficult under pressure. Good tension is observable and gives the composition a job.

## 3. Emotional target

Choose the feeling the interface should earn: grounded confidence, alert calm, or deliberate momentum. Avoid generic adjectives such as “modern.”

## 4. Domain objects and materials

List objects users already handle and materials that belong to the domain. These become evidence in the scene graph, not decoration.

## 5. Central metaphor

Choose a visual behavior that explains the product: a kitchen pass, a field ledger, a signal route, a material archive, or another original concept. Never instruct the provider to copy a company or website.

## 6. Composition

Derive the focal point, hierarchy, rhythm, alignment, and responsive rules from the metaphor. A split hero is valid only when the domain narrative requires it.

## 7. Visual system

Define color roles, type character, materials, shape language, motion principles, and forbidden patterns. The refusal list is part of the contract.

## 8. Preview and handoff

Compile the typed scene graph to deterministic HTML/CSS/SVG, lint it, and export the Markdown package. The coding agent receives decisions and constraints rather than another vague prompt.
